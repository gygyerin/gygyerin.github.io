abstract: >-
  Previous studies have established that perfluorooctanoic acid (PFOA) reduction and decomposition in UV/sulfite systems proceed via stepwise chain reactions along the decarboxylation–hydroxylation–elimination–hydrolysis (DHEH) pathway, in which hydrated electrons (eaq−) play a central role in initiating and sustaining the reaction. In contrast, the present study demonstrates that hydroxide ions (OH−) directly participate in the reductive decomposition of PFOA in a manner comparable to eaq− and act as key promoters of the degradation process. When OH− was added under buffered conditions that minimized pH variation, PFOA removal increased across the entire tested pH range (7.2–12.0). This enhancement persisted despite strict pH control, indicating that OH− contributes to PFOA degradation beyond simple bulk pH regulation and enhanced eaq− production. Analysis of intermediates and DFT calculations revealed a reduction pathway distinct from the renowned DHEH mechanism. This newly identified pathway is thermodynamically favorable (ΔG(R-4)’ = −19.27 kcal/mol) and exhibits an exceptionally low activation barrier (ΔG≠(R-4)’ = −1.04 kcal/mol), thereby enabling rapid chain-propagating defluorination. Collectively, these findings identify OH− as a principal driver of PFOA degradation and establish a new conceptual framework for UV-based reductive treatment processes. Furthermore, the results underscore the importance of elucidating OH−-activated synergistic effects to achieve more continuous and complete degradation of per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances.
